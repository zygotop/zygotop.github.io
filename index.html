We are an informal pedagogical seminar concerning algebraic topology and homotopy theory, localized at Harvard and MIT.     • Mar 05 2025

      Howard Beck

    Chromatic blueshift and redshift are two phenomena that tend to happen in stable homotopy theory, moving ring spectra up and down between the chromatic layers. Namely, blueshift happens by passing spectra to the Borel-equivariant category with trivial actions and taking geometric fixed points, while redshift happens by taking the algebraic K-theory. We will first talk about how these were discovered, out of studying stable homotopy groups of spheres and the Lichtenbaum–Quillen conjectures. Afterwards, we will state what is known about both, specializing on \(\mathbb{E}_{\infty} \)-rings where we fully understand redshift. The shifting phenomena we know are effectively governed by descent results, studying Lubin-Tate theories, and computations of the Balmer spectrum of compact genuine G-equivariant spectra. If time allows, we will talk about the “half” of blueshift that is open, and discuss why it is particularly inaccessible.

    References:

    • The Chromatic Nullstellensatz by Burklund, Schlank, and Yuan.
    • The Balmer spectrum of the equivariant homotopy category of a finite abelian group by Barthel, Hausmann, Naumann, Nikolaus, Noel, and Stapleton.
    • Examples of chromatic redshift in algebraic K-theory by Yuan.
    • Descent and vanishing in chromatic algebraic K-theory via group actions by Clausen, Mathew, Naumann, and Noel.
